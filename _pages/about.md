I am a Chartered Landscape Architect and researcher with over 10 years of professional practice, 
currently completing a PhD at the University of Edinburgh's Advanced Care Research Centre. My work 
sits at the intersection of landscape design, environmental psychology, and public health — 
examining how designed and natural environments shape wellbeing, behaviour, and quality of life.

My research applies both quantitative and qualitative methods, including statistical modelling in 
R and SPSS, thematic analysis in NVivo, and participatory co-design approaches. I have published 
in high-impact journals including *Age and Ageing* (IF 9.5) and *PLoS One*, and have presented 
findings at international environmental research conferences.

Alongside research, I hold a Teaching Fellowship at the University of Edinburgh's Centre for Data, 
Culture and Society, where I deliver training in geospatial analysis, data visualisation, and 
computational tools — including R, GIS, and AutoCAD — to over 100 academics and students. I also 
supervise MSc dissertations in Landscape and Wellbeing, with 75% of students achieving a Merit or 
higher.

In professional practice, I work on infrastructure and environmental impact projects, including 
landscape and visual impact assessment for major energy transmission schemes. I have also led 
research consultancy for organisations including Creative Scotland and the Edinburgh Climate Change 
Institute, where my work has informed grant proposals and projects valued at over £1.5 million.

My broader interests span geospatial data science, person-centred design, and the application of 
computational tools to landscape and health research.
