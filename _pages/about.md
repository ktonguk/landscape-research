I am a Chartered Landscape Architect and researcher with over 10 years of professional practice, currently completing a PhD at the University of Edinburgh's Advanced Care Research Centre. My work sits at the intersection of landscape design, environmental psychology, and public health — examining how designed and natural environments shape wellbeing, behaviour, and quality of life.

My research applies both quantitative and qualitative methods, including statistical modelling in R and SPSS, thematic analysis in NVivo, and participatory co-design approaches. I have published in high-impact journals including *Age and Ageing* (IF 9.5) and *PLoS One*, and have presented findings at international environmental research conferences, including GISRUK, EDRA56 and IAPS.

Alongside research, I hold a position as a Teaching Fellow at the University of Edinburgh's Centre for Data, Culture and Society, where I deliver training in geospatial analysis, data visualisation, and computational tools to over 100 academics and students. I also supervise MSc dissertations in Landscape and Wellbeing, with 75% of students achieving a Merit or higher.

In professional practice, I work on infrastructure and environmental impact projects, including landscape and visual impact assessment for major energy transmission schemes. I have delivered research work for organisations including Landscape Institute, Creative Scotland and the Edinburgh Climate Change Institute.

My broader interests span geospatial data science, person-centred design, and the application of computational tools to landscape and health research.
